Urban Noise

Urban noise in a design context refers to the complex auditory environment shaped by human activities within city settings. It encompasses various sound sources, such as traffic, construction, and social interactions, impacting the acoustic landscape.
Designers address urban noise to enhance the quality of public spaces, considering factors like soundscapes, architectural acoustics, and strategic placement of elements. Mitigation strategies may involve incorporating sound-absorbing materials, green spaces, and thoughtful urban planning to create environments fostering tranquility and well-being. Urban noise management in design aims for a harmonious coexistence of city life and environmental sensitivity, fostering spaces that balance functionality with auditory comfort.
